Gatrointestinal Cancer

This type of cancer is mainly caused by a genetic mutation in the cell of  the stomach . This mutation abets a quick and abnormal growth of the cell which eventually morphs into a tumor. There are various causes of Gatrointestinal cancer and the major causes are listed below

Family History 

Up to 10% of gastrointestinal cancer cases are genetically associated. Research shows that "Among individuals with a family history,​current or past Helicobacter pylori infection, having two or more first-degree affected relatives or female gender was associated with an increased risk of developing gastric cancer."

Smoking

Research shows that smoking increases the odds of gastrointestinalc cancer by 61%

A diet high in salt and severely lacking in fruits and vegeatables increases the risk of an individual developing gastrointestinal cancer. Obesity is also a major cause of this cancer.
